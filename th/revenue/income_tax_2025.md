## Summary
Thailand taxes resident individuals on a **progressive 0%–35%** scale (top rate **35%** on net income above **฿5,000,000**). Employers **must withhold PIT monthly** from salary (computed on annualised projected income) and remit it on **Form PND.1 (ภ.ง.ด.1)** within **7 days after month-end**; they issue each employee a **withholding tax certificate (Form 50 Bis / 50 ทวิ)** by **15 February**. Net (taxable) income = gross employment income − a **50% expense deduction (capped ฿100,000)** − personal allowances (personal ฿60,000, spouse ฿60,000, child ฿30,000 / ฿60,000).

> Currency: Thai Baht (**฿ / THB**). Tax authority: **Revenue Department** (กรมสรรพากร). Tax year = calendar year.

## Progressive PIT rates (net/taxable income)
| Net income (฿) | Marginal rate | Tax (cumulative) |
|----------------|:------------:|------------------|
| 0 – 150,000 | Exempt | 0 |
| 150,001 – 300,000 | 5% | 5% of excess over 150,000 |
| 300,001 – 500,000 | 10% | 7,500 + 10% of excess over 300,000 |
| 500,001 – 750,000 | 15% | 27,500 + 15% of excess over 500,000 |
| 750,001 – 1,000,000 | 20% | 65,000 + 20% of excess over 750,000 |
| 1,000,001 – 2,000,000 | 25% | 115,000 + 25% of excess over 1,000,000 |
| 2,000,001 – 5,000,000 | 30% | 365,000 + 30% of excess over 2,000,000 |
| Over 5,000,000 | **35%** | 1,265,000 + 35% of excess over 5,000,000 |

## Key deductions & allowances (employment income)
| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Expense deduction (employment income) | **50% of income, capped at ฿100,000** |
| Personal allowance | **฿60,000** |
| Spouse allowance (spouse has no income) | **฿60,000** |
| Child allowance — 1st child | **฿30,000** |
| Child allowance — 2nd & subsequent child **born in/after 2018** | **฿60,000** |
| Social Security Fund employee contributions | Deductible (see [`../sso/social_security.md`](../sso/social_security.md)) |
| Provident fund / RMF / SSF / Thai ESG, life & health insurance, mortgage interest, parental care | Additional reliefs apply — **confirm exact caps against the RD Allowances & Exemptions attachment** |

**Net income = gross employment income − 50%/฿100,000 expense − allowances above.** Apply the bracket table to net income.

## Tax residency
- **Resident** if present in Thailand **≥ 180 days** in the tax (calendar) year.
- Residents are taxed on Thai-source income, and on **foreign-source income remitted to Thailand** (income earned from **1 Jan 2024** onward, taxed in the year remitted — the 2024 rule change).

---

## Monthly employer withholding (PND.1)
- The employer **annualises** the employee's projected income, computes annual tax on the progressive scale, and withholds a proportional slice **each pay period** (Revenue Code s.50(1)).
- **Remit on Form PND.1 (ภ.ง.ด.1) within 7 days after the end of the month of payment** (s.52). (RD e-filing typically extends this by ~8 days — confirm the current e-filing deadline.)
- **Withholding tax certificate — Form 50 Bis (50 ทวิ):** issue to each employee **by 15 February** of the following year (or within one month of employment ending) — s.50 Bis.
- **Annual employer return — PND.1 Kor (ภ.ง.ด.1ก):** summary of all employees' remuneration and tax withheld, filed early in the year (commonly by end of February; the duty is s.58/59).
- The employee's own annual return (PND.91) is filed **1 Jan – 31 March** (e-filing to ~8 April).
