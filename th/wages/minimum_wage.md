## Summary
Thailand's minimum wage is set **per province as a daily rate** by the Wage Committee. Under the current **Notification No. 14** (effective **1 July 2025**, replacing No. 13), the geographic rate ranges from **฿337 to ฿400 per day** across 17 tiers. **฿400** applies in **Bangkok** (raised from ฿372 by No. 14), **Chachoengsao, Chon Buri, Phuket, Rayong, and Koh Samui district (Surat Thani)**. No. 14 also adds a **nationwide ฿400 rule** for **hotels (types 2/3/4)** and **entertainment/service venues**, regardless of province. The lowest **฿337** applies in Narathiwat, Pattani, and Yala.

> Currency: **฿ / THB**. Set by the **Wage Committee** under the Ministry of Labour.

> `volatility: continuously-changing` — provincial daily rates are revised by Wage Committee notification roughly annually. **Re-pull the latest notification before relying on a rate.** (No. 14 replaced No. 13 on 1 Jul 2025; the only geographic change was Bangkok ฿372→฿400, plus the new sector rule.)
