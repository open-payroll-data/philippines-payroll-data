## Summary
Indonesian employees get at least **12 days paid annual leave** after 12 months of service, and a **mandatory THR** (Tunjangan Hari Raya — religious-holiday allowance): **one month's wage** for ≥12 months' service (pro-rata for 1–<12 months), paid **at least 7 days before** the religious holiday. Baseline **maternity leave is 3 months** at full pay; the **2024 UU KIA** (UU 4/2024) allows extension to **up to 6 months** in special health conditions, paid **100% for months 1–4 then 75% for months 5–6**, and gives fathers **2 days** (extendable to ~3).

> Currency: **Rp / IDR**. Statutes: UU 13/2003 (as amended); Permenaker 6/2016; PP 36/2021; **UU No. 4/2024 (UU KIA)**.

## Annual leave (UU 13/2003 Pasal 79)
**At least 12 working days** paid, after **12 months** of continuous service. The detailed scheme is set in the employment contract / company regulation / CBA but cannot fall below 12 days.

## THR — Tunjangan Hari Raya (mandatory)
| Service | THR |
|---------|-----|
| ≥ 12 months | **1 month's wage** |
| 1 – < 12 months | **(months of service ÷ 12) × 1 month's wage** (pro-rata) |

- Paid **at least 7 days before** the religious holiday (Permenaker 6/2016 Pasal 5).
- Wage = basic + fixed allowance. Late/non-payment penalty: **5%** of the THR (PP 36/2021), plus sanctions. The government issues an annual Menaker circular (SE) restating the rules before Idul Fitri.

## Maternity & paternity leave
| Leave | Rule |
|-------|------|
| Maternity — baseline (UU 13/2003 Pasal 82) | **3 months** (1.5 before + 1.5 after birth), full pay |
| Maternity — UU KIA (UU 4/2024) extension | up to **6 months** if a special post-partum health condition is certified; pay **100% for months 1–4, 75% for months 5–6** |
| Miscarriage | 1.5 months (per doctor's note) |
| Paternity (UU KIA) | **2 days**, extendable up to **3 more** by agreement |

> UU KIA (UU No. 4/2024) was signed **2 July 2024** (LN.2024 No. 98) and is in force. It sets a *minimum* 3-month maternity leave with the conditional extension above; the 3-month full-pay baseline under UU 13/2003 remains the floor.
