## Summary
Malaysia taxes resident individuals on a **progressive 0%–30%** scale (top rate **30%** on chargeable income above **RM2,000,000**), unchanged for YA2023–YA2025. Unlike Singapore, Malaysia **has mandatory monthly employer withholding** — **MTD/PCB** (Monthly Tax Deduction / *Potongan Cukai Bulanan*) — remitted by the **15th of the following month**, and it can be **final tax**. **Non-resident** employment income is taxed at a **flat 30%** (no personal reliefs). Employers file **Form E** (annual, by 31 March) and issue **Form EA** to each employee by the **end of February**.

> Currency: Malaysian Ringgit (**RM**). Tax authority: **LHDN / IRBM** (Lembaga Hasil Dalam Negeri / Inland Revenue Board of Malaysia). Year of Assessment (YA) = calendar year.

## Resident income tax rates — YA2024 & YA2025
LHDN publishes one table for YA2023–YA2025; the employment-income scale is **identical** across those years. Tax = (cumulative tax on the band floor) + (rate × income above the floor).

| Chargeable income (RM) | Rate on band | Tax on first (cumulative, RM) |
|------------------------|:-----------:|:-----------------------------:|
| 0 – 5,000 | 0% | 0 |
| 5,001 – 20,000 | 1% | 150 |
| 20,001 – 35,000 | 3% | 600 |
| 35,001 – 50,000 | 6% | 1,500 |
| 50,001 – 70,000 | 11% | 3,700 |
| 70,001 – 100,000 | 19% | 9,400 |
| 100,001 – 400,000 | 25% | 84,400 |
| 400,001 – 600,000 | 26% | 136,400 |
| 600,001 – 2,000,000 | 28% | 528,400 |
| Exceeding 2,000,000 | **30%** | — |

**Top marginal rate: 30%**, on chargeable income above RM2,000,000.

> **Budget 2025 note:** the employment-income scale did **not** change. The widely reported Budget 2025 measure is a **separate 2% tax on individual dividend income above RM100,000** (YA2025) — it does **not** affect salary or MTD/PCB.

## Non-resident employment income
- **Residency:** non-resident if present in Malaysia **< 182 days** in the calendar year (regardless of nationality).
- **Employment income: flat 30%** (YA2020 onward); **no personal reliefs**.
- **60-day exemption:** a non-resident exercising employment in Malaysia for **≤ 60 days** is exempt on that employment income.

---

## Monthly Tax Deduction (MTD / PCB)
- Malaysia **requires employers to deduct income tax monthly** from remuneration — **MTD/PCB**, governed by the **Income Tax (Deduction from Remuneration) Rules 1994** (as amended). Computed via the official MTD schedule or the **Computerised Calculation Method** (annual PCB specification).
- **Remittance deadline: on or before the 15th of the following month.**
- **CP38:** additional deductions ordered by LHDN (e.g. to recover tax arrears) are remitted **on top of** the normal MTD.
- **MTD as final tax:** an employee may elect **not to file** an annual return if all conditions are met — only employment income under s.13 ITA 1967, a **single employer**, MTD correctly deducted, and no separate-assessment election.

## Employer obligations & forms
| Obligation | Form | Deadline |
|------------|------|----------|
| Register as employer | E (number) | On becoming an employer |
| **Remit monthly MTD/PCB** | — | **By the 15th of the following month** |
| Employer annual return + employee listing | **Form E + C.P.8D** | **By 31 March** of the following year (mandatory e-Filing) |
| Employee statement of remuneration | **Form EA** (private sector) | **By the last day of February** of the following year |
| New employee notification | **CP22** | Within **30 days** of commencement (e-CP22 via MyTax mandatory from 1 Sep 2024) |
| Cessation of employment | **CP22A** | **≥ 30 days before** cessation (via e-SPC) |
| Employee leaving Malaysia > 3 months | **CP21** | **≥ 30 days before** departure |

## Key personal reliefs (YA2025, MTD-relevant — brief)
Individual & dependents **RM9,000**; **EPF + life insurance RM7,000** (mandatory EPF RM4,000 + life/takaful RM3,000); **SOCSO/EIS RM350**; lifestyle RM2,500; medical (serious illness/fertility/etc.) RM10,000; spouse (no income) RM4,000; each child < 18 **RM2,000** (RM8,000 if 18+ in higher education). Non-residents get **none** of these.
