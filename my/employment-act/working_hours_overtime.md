## Summary
Since the **2022 amendment (in force 1 Jan 2023)** the **Employment Act 1955 covers all employees** under a contract of service — but a set of wage-protection provisions (**overtime, rest-day pay, public-holiday pay, shift allowance, statutory termination benefits**) **does not apply to employees earning more than RM4,000/month**. Working hours are capped at **45 hours/week**. Overtime is **1.5×** on a normal day, **2×** for excess hours on a rest day, and **3×** for excess hours on a public holiday.

> Currency: **RM**. Scope: the EA 1955 applies to **Peninsular Malaysia + the Federal Territory of Labuan**; **Sabah** (Labour Ordinance Cap. 67) and **Sarawak** (Cap. 76) have separate laws.

## Coverage & the RM4,000 threshold
- The Act applies to **all employees** under a contract of service, regardless of wages (First Schedule para 1).
- **But** these provisions **do NOT apply** to an employee whose **wages exceed RM4,000/month** (First Schedule para 1A): **s.60(3)** rest-day pay, **s.60A(3)** overtime, **s.60C(2A)** shift allowance, **s.60D(3)/(4)** public-holiday pay, and **s.60J** termination/lay-off/retirement benefits.
- Certain provisions still apply **regardless of wages** to specified **manual-worker** categories (manual labour; operating/maintaining vehicles for transport; supervising manual workers; certain vessel crew).

**So:** statutory overtime / rest-day / public-holiday premiums and statutory termination benefits are a right only for employees earning **≤ RM4,000/month** (plus the manual categories). Above RM4,000 these are contractual only.

## Working hours (s.60A)
- **≤ 45 hours per week** (reduced from 48 by the 2022 amendment).
- **≤ 8 hours per day** (may reach **9** only under an agreement that redistributes hours so other days are shorter); spread-over **≤ 10 hours/day**.
- A rest break of **≥ 30 minutes** after no more than **5 consecutive hours** of work.

## Overtime rates
| Situation | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Normal working day — hours beyond normal | **≥ 1.5×** the hourly rate (s.60A(3)(a)) |
| Rest day — within normal hours | ½-day to 2-days' wages by hours worked (s.60(3)(a)/(b) day-wage table) |
| Rest day — hours in excess of normal | **≥ 2×** the hourly rate (s.60(3)(c)) |
| Public holiday — work done (normal hours) | **+2 days' wages** at the ordinary rate, on top of holiday pay (s.60D(3)) |
| Public holiday — hours in excess of normal | **≥ 3×** the hourly rate (s.60D(3)(aa)) |
