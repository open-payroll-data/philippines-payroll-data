## Summary
Philippine statutory leaves: **Service Incentive Leave** = 5 days/yr (Labor Code Art. 95); **Maternity** = **105 days** paid (RA 11210, +15 if solo parent, +30 unpaid option, 60 for miscarriage); **Paternity** = 7 days (RA 8187); **Solo-parent** = 7 days/yr (RA 8972 as amended by RA 11861); **Special leave for women** = up to 2 months after gynecological surgery (RA 9710); **VAWC** = up to 10 days (RA 9262). **Only the maternity cash benefit is paid by SSS** (the employer tops up a salary differential) — every other leave here is **employer-paid**.

## ✅ Verification & provenance
- **Verified against primary law texts** on lawphil.net (the cited RAs + Labor Code Art. 95), read 2026-06-25 (`source_tier: primary`).
- **Two nuances commonly gotten wrong** (both confirmed here): **(1)** solo-parent leave eligibility is **6 months** of service, **not 1 year**, and the leave is *forfeitable & non-cumulative* (RA 11861); **(2)** the maternity **salary differential is income-tax EXEMPT** along with the SSS benefit, per **BIR RMC 105-2019** — some summaries wrongly tax it.
- **Caveat:** the maternity tax-exemption rests on BIR **RMC 105-2019** (cited, not separately PDF-rendered this pass). A 30-day paternity leave and a national menstrual leave are **pending bills only** — do not treat as law.

## Detail & nuances

### Service Incentive Leave (SIL) — Labor Code Art. 95
5 days with pay per year after 1 year of service. **Convertible to cash** if unused at year-end. **Exempt:** employers with **< 10 employees**; those already granting ≥5 days paid vacation leave; field personnel / paid-by-results; managerial employees. *(Cash conversion of unused SIL counts toward the ₱90,000 "13th-month + other benefits" tax-exempt ceiling — see [`../benefits/13th_month_pay.md`](../benefits/13th_month_pay.md).)*

### Maternity — RA 11210 (105-Day Expanded Maternity Leave Law)
- **105 days full pay** for live childbirth (any delivery mode); **+15** if a qualified solo parent; **+30 unpaid** at the worker's option; **60 days** for miscarriage/emergency termination.
- Covers **all** female workers — government, private, informal economy, voluntary/self-employed SSS members — regardless of civil status or the child's legitimacy.
- **The mother may allocate up to 7 days** of her leave to the **child's father** (married or not), or to an alternate caregiver — this is **on top of** the father's RA 8187 paternity leave (so a father may effectively get up to **14 days**).
- **Payment (private sector) is split:** **SSS** pays the cash maternity benefit; the **employer advances** it and is **reimbursed 100%** by SSS; the **employer separately pays the "salary differential"** so the employee receives her **full salary**. Salary-differential **exemptions:** distressed establishments, retail/service with **≤10 workers**, micro-business (assets ≤₱3M), or those already granting equal/better benefits.
- **Tax:** **both** the SSS maternity benefit **and** the employer salary differential are **income-tax exempt** (BIR **RMC 105-2019**); the differential is a deductible employer expense.

### Paternity — RA 8187
**7 days** with full pay, employer-paid, for a **married** male employee cohabiting with his spouse, for the **first 4 deliveries** ("delivery" includes childbirth or miscarriage).

### Solo-parent (parental) leave — RA 8972 as amended by RA 11861
**7 working days per year**, with pay, employer-paid — **forfeitable and non-cumulative**. Eligibility: **at least 6 months** of service (RA 11861 lowered this from 1 year) and a **Solo Parent ID** (valid 1 year). RA 11861 also broadened the **definition** of solo parent and other benefits (e.g., possible discounts/monthly subsidy via LGUs).

### Special leave for women — RA 9710 (Magna Carta of Women) §18
**Up to 2 months** with full pay (based on gross monthly compensation) **following surgery for a gynecological disorder**; requires ≥6 months aggregate service in the last 12 months. Employer-paid; **not convertible to cash, not cumulative.**

### VAWC leave — RA 9262 §43
**Up to 10 days** of paid leave (extendible per a protection order), **in addition to** other paid leaves, for a woman employee who is a VAWC victim, upon certification that a VAWC action is pending. Employer-paid; not cumulative/convertible.
