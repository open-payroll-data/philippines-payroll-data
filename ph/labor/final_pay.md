## Summary
Per **DOLE Labor Advisory No. 06-2020**, an employer must release an employee's **final pay within 30 days** of separation (unless a more favorable company policy/CBA applies), and issue a **Certificate of Employment within 3 days** of request. **Final pay** is the sum of all amounts due regardless of the cause of separation (unpaid salary, cash conversion of unused SIL, pro-rated 13th-month pay, separation/retirement pay if applicable, tax refund). **Separation pay** is only due for **authorized causes** (Labor Code Arts. 298–299), not for just-cause dismissals.

## Final pay — the 30-day rule
**Release within 30 days** from the date of separation/termination, **unless** a company policy, CBA, or individual agreement sets a shorter/more favorable period (LA 06-2020). Employers may run **reasonable clearance** procedures and offset **due** accountabilities, but that does not excuse non-payment beyond the period. Disputes go to the nearest **DOLE Regional/Field Office** for conciliation.

### Components of final pay
| Component | Basis |
|-----------|-------|
| Unpaid earned salary up to the separation date | Labor Code Art. 103 |
| Cash conversion of **unused Service Incentive Leave** (≥5 days/yr if eligible) | Labor Code Art. 95 |
| Cash conversion of other unused leaves (VL/SL) | Company policy / CBA |
| **Pro-rated 13th-month pay** (basic salary earned that year ÷ 12) | PD 851 |
| **Separation pay**, if applicable | Labor Code Arts. 298–299 |
| **Retirement pay**, if qualified | Labor Code Art. 302 (RA 7641) |
| **Income-tax refund** for excess withheld tax (year-end/separation adjustment) | NIRC |
| Returnable **cash bonds/deposits** | Labor Code Art. 114 |
| Other amounts due under contract/CBA/policy | Respective agreement |

## Certificate of Employment (COE)
Issued **within 3 days** of the employee's request (LA 06-2020). States only the **dates of employment** and the **position(s) held** — no performance evaluation required. A COE is **not** the same as a clearance or a quitclaim.

## Separation pay — when due and the rates (Arts. 298–299)
Separation pay is due for **authorized causes** (business/health grounds, not employee fault). It is **not** required for **just-cause** dismissals (serious misconduct, fraud, etc., Art. 297) unless a policy/CBA grants it.

| Authorized cause | Separation pay |
|------------------|----------------|
| Installation of **labor-saving devices** | **1 month** pay **or 1 month per year of service**, whichever is higher |
| **Redundancy** | **1 month** pay **or 1 month per year of service**, whichever is higher |
| **Retrenchment** to prevent losses | **1 month** pay **or ½ month per year of service**, whichever is higher |
| **Closure/cessation** (not due to serious losses) | **1 month** pay **or ½ month per year of service**, whichever is higher |
| Closure due to **serious business losses** | **None** required |
| **Disease** (continued employment prohibited/prejudicial) | **1 month** salary **or ½ month per year of service**, whichever is greater |

- A **fraction of ≥6 months** of service counts as **one whole year**.
- **Notice:** for Art. 298 causes, written notice to **the worker and DOLE at least 30 days** before the effective date. Disease (Art. 299) requires a competent public-health certification that it can't be cured within 6 months. Implementing rules: DOLE **D.O. 147-15**.
- **Tax:** separation pay for causes **beyond the employee's control** (authorized causes, death, sickness) is **income-tax exempt** under the NIRC — a reason a tax refund often appears in final pay.
