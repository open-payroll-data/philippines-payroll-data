## Summary
RA 10963 (TRAIN Law) took effect January 1, 2018 and is the primary law governing income tax on employees in the Philippines. It has two tranches of tax rate reductions: a first tranche effective 2018, and a second (lower) tranche effective 2023. For payroll purposes, the 2023+ rates are what apply today.

## Key changes TRAIN made for payroll

### 1. Lower income tax rates
Old rate (before TRAIN): 5%–32% graduated
New rate (2018–2022): 0%–35% graduated (lower middle rates)
New rate (2023+): 0%–35% graduated (further reduced lower brackets)
See [`income_tax_table_2025.md`](income_tax_table_2025.md) for the full tables.

### 2. Zero tax for minimum wage earners (unchanged)
All minimum wage earners remain fully exempt — including overtime pay, holiday pay, and night shift differential. This was not a TRAIN change but is preserved under TRAIN.

### 3. Removed personal exemptions
TRAIN **eliminated** personal exemptions (₱50,000) and additional exemptions for dependents (₱25,000 per child, up to 4). As of 2018, no more exemptions of this type. Compensation exemption for MWEs remains.

### 4. Increased 13th month + bonus tax-exempt ceiling
Before TRAIN: ₱82,000 cap for 13th month pay and bonuses
After TRAIN: ₱90,000 cap (combined: 13th month + other bonuses + productivity incentives, etc.)
See [`../benefits/13th_month_pay.md`](../benefits/13th_month_pay.md).

### 5. Updated de minimis benefit ceilings
TRAIN raised some de minimis ceilings (BIR Revenue Regulations No. 11-2018). **These were further raised by BIR RR 29-2025, effective 6 January 2026.**
See [`../benefits/de_minimis_benefits.md`](../benefits/de_minimis_benefits.md) for the current amounts.

### 6. SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG contributions still deductible
Employee mandatory government contributions remain deductible from gross compensation in computing taxable income. This is unchanged.

## Two tranches at a glance

| Period | Notable change |
|--------|---------------|
| Jan 2018 – Dec 2022 | First rate cut: e.g., ₱250K–₱400K bracket moved from 25% to 20% |
| Jan 2023 onward | Second rate cut: same bracket moved to 15%; additional bracket reductions |

The 2023+ rates are permanently in effect (no sunset clause). No further changes announced as of 2026.

---

## What TRAIN did NOT change for payroll

- Mandatory 13th month pay obligation (still PD 851)
- Minimum wage exemption
- SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG contribution deductibility
- BIR Form 2316 (Certificate of Compensation — still required)
- BIR Form 1604-C (Annual Alphalist — still required)
