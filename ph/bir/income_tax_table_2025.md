## Summary
The TRAIN Law (RA 10963) has two rate tranches: 2018–2022 and 2023 onward. The 2023+ rates apply through at least 2025. Employees earning ₱250,000 or less annually pay **zero income tax**. Rates rise to 35% for earnings above ₱8 million.

These tables are used for annualized withholding tax computation (the "annualization method" required by BIR).

## Annual Taxable Income Tax Table (2023 onward)

| Annual Taxable Income | Tax Due |
|----------------------|---------|
| Not over ₱250,000 | 0% (exempt) |
| Over ₱250,000 but not over ₱400,000 | 15% of excess over ₱250,000 |
| Over ₱400,000 but not over ₱800,000 | ₱22,500 + 20% of excess over ₱400,000 |
| Over ₱800,000 but not over ₱2,000,000 | ₱102,500 + 25% of excess over ₱800,000 |
| Over ₱2,000,000 but not over ₱8,000,000 | ₱402,500 + 30% of excess over ₱2,000,000 |
| Over ₱8,000,000 | ₱2,202,500 + 35% of excess over ₱8,000,000 |

## What counts as "taxable income"

Taxable compensation income = gross compensation MINUS:
- Non-taxable de minimis benefits (within BIR ceilings — see `../benefits/de_minimis_benefits.md`)
- 13th month pay and other bonuses — first ₱90,000 is exempt (see `../benefits/13th_month_pay.md`)
- SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG employee contributions (these are deductible)
- Personal exemption: NONE (TRAIN removed personal and additional exemptions)

**Minimum wage earners are fully exempt** from income tax (including their holiday pay, overtime pay, night-shift differential, and hazard pay — per RR 8-2018).

## Quick example

Employee monthly basic salary: ₱40,000
- SSS employee share (approx): ₱900
- PhilHealth employee share: ₱1,000 (5% of ₱40,000 ÷ 2)
- Pag-IBIG: ₱100
- Total deductions: ₱2,000
- Monthly taxable income: ₱38,000

Tax on ₱38,000 using monthly table:
= ₱1,875 + 20% × (₱38,000 − ₱33,333)
= ₱1,875 + 20% × ₱4,667
= ₱1,875 + ₱933.40
= **₱2,808.40 monthly withholding tax**
