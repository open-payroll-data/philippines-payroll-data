## Summary
Presidential Decree 851 requires all rank-and-file private sector employees to receive a 13th month pay of **at least 1/12 of total basic salary** earned during the calendar year. It must be paid **on or before December 24**. The first **₱90,000** of 13th month pay plus other bonuses combined is **income-tax exempt** under TRAIN Law.

## Who is covered

**Required to receive 13th month pay:**
- All rank-and-file private sector employees regardless of designation or employment status
- Includes: probationary, project-based, seasonal, part-time employees
- As long as they have worked **at least one month** in the calendar year

**NOT covered by PD 851 (but may have similar benefits by contract or CBA):**
- Government employees (covered by separate rules)
- Managerial employees (expressly exempt from PD 851)
- Household workers / kasambahay (covered separately under RA 10361 / Kasambahay Law — may receive 13th month too; check)
- Employees paid purely on commission or boundary basis where no employer-employee relationship exists

## How to compute

### Basic formula
```
13th month pay = (Total Basic Salary Earned During the Year) ÷ 12
```

**"Total basic salary earned" includes:** Regular basic pay for days actually worked or on paid leave.
**Excludes:** Overtime pay, holiday premium, night differential, cost of living allowances, profit-sharing, and any monetary benefits not considered part of basic pay under the employment contract or CBA.

### For employees who did not work the full year
Same formula still applies — you divide the actual basic salary earned by 12 (not by months worked). This naturally prorates to what they earned.

### Example
- Employee basic salary: ₱25,000/month
- Worked full 12 months (no absences)
- Total basic salary = ₱25,000 × 12 = ₱300,000
- 13th month pay = ₱300,000 ÷ 12 = **₱25,000**

### Example: partial year (hired March 15)
- Monthly basic: ₱25,000
- Worked 9.5 months (mid-March to December 31)
- Basic salary earned ≈ ₱237,500 (9.5 × ₱25,000; adjust for actual days)
- 13th month pay = ₱237,500 ÷ 12 = **₱19,791.67**

## Deadline

| Requirement | Deadline |
|-------------|----------|
| 13th month pay (full amount) | On or before December 24 of each year |
| May be split: 50% before May 15 (mid-year) + 50% before December 24 | Optional, both halves still total at least 1/12 |

Many employers pay the full amount in December or alongside November/December payroll.

---

## Tax treatment

| Amount | Tax treatment |
|--------|--------------|
| First ₱90,000 (combined: 13th month + other bonuses + productivity incentives + Christmas gifts + other similar benefits) | **Income-tax EXEMPT** |
| Amount exceeding ₱90,000 | Taxable — added to regular compensation and subjected to graduated income tax |

**Key point:** The ₱90,000 ceiling is for ALL similar benefits combined in the year — not just the 13th month alone. If an employee also received a mid-year bonus, performance bonus, and Christmas gift, all of them count against the same ₱90,000 ceiling.

**Benefits covered by the ₱90,000 ceiling:**
- 13th month pay
- Christmas bonus / 14th month pay
- Mid-year bonus
- Performance bonus / productivity incentive
- Other benefits of a similar nature

---

## Employer obligations

1. Pay at least the minimum (1/12 of basic salary earned) — paying more is allowed
2. Pay on or before December 24
3. Reflect the 13th month pay in the employee's payslip and annual BIR Form 2316
4. Track the ₱90,000 ceiling across all bonuses for annualization
