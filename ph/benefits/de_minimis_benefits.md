## Summary
De minimis benefits are small amounts given to employees that the BIR treats as non-taxable, up to specified ceilings. They are **exempt from income tax AND fringe benefit tax**. Amounts that **exceed** the ceilings are taxable compensation income and must be added back to the employee's taxable pay. **BIR RR 29-2025 raised these ceilings effective 6 January 2026** (e.g. rice ₱2,000 → ₱2,500/month; uniform ₱6,000 → ₱8,000/year); the amounts below are the current RR 29-2025 figures.

## BIR De Minimis Benefits and Ceilings (current — per RR 29-2025, effective 6 Jan 2026)

| # | Benefit | Non-taxable Ceiling | Notes |
|---|---------|---------------------|-------|
| 1 | Monetized unused vacation leave credits | Up to **12 days/year** | Private-sector employees, at the current daily rate (raised from 10 by RR 29-2025) |
| 2 | Monetized unused sick leave credits | Government employees: exempt (with VL). Private sector: **not** a separate de minimis item | Only vacation leave (row 1) qualifies for private-sector employees |
| 3 | Medical cash allowance to dependents | **₱2,000/employee/semester** | For the employee's dependents; medical purposes (raised from ₱1,500) |
| 4 | Rice subsidy | **₱2,500/month** OR one 50kg sack of rice/month | Cash max ₱2,500; or one sack ≤ ₱2,500 (raised from ₱2,000) |
| 5 | Uniform and clothing allowance | **₱8,000/year** | For uniforms required by the employer (raised from ₱6,000) |
| 6 | Actual medical assistance | **₱12,000/year** | Cash assistance for the employee's own medical needs (raised from ₱10,000) |
| 7 | Laundry allowance | **₱400/month** | Must be related to work-uniform laundering (raised from ₱300) |
| 8 | Employee achievement awards (tangible, non-cash) | **₱12,000/year** | Length-of-service or safety achievement; non-cash only (raised from ₱10,000) |
| 9 | Gifts given in celebration of Christmas, major anniversary | **₱6,000/year** | Cash or gift checks; for the specific occasion (raised from ₱5,000) |
| 10 | Daily meal allowance for overtime and night/graveyard shift | **30% of applicable minimum wage** per region | Per day of actual OT/night-shift work (raised from 25%) |
| 11 | CBA benefits + productivity incentive schemes (combined) | **₱12,000/year** | Combined annual ceiling (raised from ₱10,000) |

## How to apply the ceilings

For each benefit, only the amount **within** the ceiling is non-taxable. Any excess is added to the employee's taxable compensation for withholding tax purposes.

### Example: Rice subsidy
- Employee receives ₱3,000/month in rice cash allowance
- Non-taxable portion: ₱2,500/month (the RR 29-2025 ceiling)
- Excess: ₱500/month → this ₱500 is **taxable compensation** and must be included in monthly taxable income
- *Note: a ₱2,500/month rice allowance is now **fully exempt** — under the old ₱2,000 ceiling, ₱500 of it was taxable.*

### Example: Uniform allowance
- Employee receives ₱9,000/year uniform allowance
- Non-taxable portion: ₱8,000 (the RR 29-2025 ceiling)
- Excess: ₱1,000 → taxable compensation

---

## De minimis vs the ₱90,000 ceiling — they are different

| Category | What's included | Tax treatment |
|----------|----------------|--------------|
| De minimis benefits | Rice, laundry, clothing, medical, gifts (listed above) | Non-taxable up to each item's ceiling; excess is regular taxable income |
| ₱90,000 ceiling | 13th month pay + performance bonuses + productivity incentives + similar cash benefits | First ₱90,000 total is exempt; excess is taxable |

De minimis benefits are **NOT** applied against the ₱90,000 ceiling. They are entirely separate exemptions.

---

## Important rules

1. **Must be given in cash or kind** — a theoretical or notional benefit doesn't qualify
2. **Must be given uniformly to all employees** or to a group of employees under a reasonable policy — a benefit given only to a specific individual may be reclassified as taxable
3. **The entire benefit is non-taxable up to the ceiling** — there's no partial non-taxability within the ceiling
4. **Excess is fully taxable** as compensation income (NOT as fringe benefit for rank-and-file; fringe benefit tax applies only to managerial/supervisory)
5. **Keep documentation** — employers should have payroll records showing the benefit amount given per employee

---

## Fringe Benefit Tax (FBT) — only for managerial/supervisory, not for rank-and-file

De minimis rules apply to **all employees**. However:
- Rank-and-file employees: excess de minimis → added to taxable compensation → withholding tax
- Managerial/supervisory employees: any non-de-minimis fringe benefit → subject to Fringe Benefit Tax (32% on grossed-up monetary value), NOT part of withholding tax computation

This file covers rank-and-file. FBT is a separate topic.
