## Summary
Under Philippine law, certain compensation items are fully or partially exempt from income tax. This file consolidates all non-taxable categories. Three main buckets: (1) full exemptions regardless of amount, (2) de minimis benefits up to BIR ceilings, and (3) the ₱90,000 annual ceiling for 13th month and similar bonuses.

## Category 1 — Fully exempt regardless of amount

These are non-taxable with no peso ceiling:

| Item | Legal basis | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Compensation of minimum wage earners (MWEs) | RA 9504, preserved in TRAIN | Entire compensation exempt: basic pay, OT, holiday pay, NSD. MWE = daily rate at regional minimum wage |
| Holiday pay of MWEs | Same | Both legal (regular/special) holiday pay |
| Overtime pay of MWEs | Same | — |
| Night shift differential pay of MWEs | Same | — |
| Hazard pay of MWEs | Same | — |
| Retirement benefits under RA 7641 | RA 7641 (Retirement Pay Law) | Must meet conditions: at least 60 years old, 5+ years of service, one-time availment |
| Retirement benefits from private pension plan approved by BIR | NIRC Section 32(B)(6)(a) | Reasonable private plan registered with BIR |
| Separation pay due to death, sickness, physical disability | NIRC Section 32(B)(6)(b) | Must be involuntary separation |
| Separation pay due to causes beyond employee's control | NIRC | Retrenchment, redundancy, closure — not voluntary resignation |
| SSS benefits, GSIS benefits, Pag-IBIG benefits, PhilHealth benefits | NIRC | Benefits received from these agencies (not contributions) |
| Benefits from the GSIS, SSS, and similar government agency | Same | Pensions, disability, death benefits |
| 13th month pay and other benefits — up to ₱90,000 | RA 10963 (TRAIN) | See separate file; excess above ₱90,000 is taxable |
| De minimis benefits — up to BIR ceilings per item | BIR RR 29-2025 (raised ceilings, eff. 2026) | See separate file; excess is taxable |
| Compensation income of barangay health workers and similar | Special laws | Check specific law |

## Category 2 — De minimis benefits (up to BIR ceilings)

Non-taxable only within the per-item ceilings. Excess is taxable compensation.

See full details in [`de_minimis_benefits.md`](de_minimis_benefits.md).

Quick reference (current ceilings per **RR 29-2025**, effective 6 Jan 2026):
- Rice subsidy: ₱2,500/month or 1 sack
- Uniform allowance: ₱8,000/year
- Medical cash assistance (employee): ₱12,000/year
- Laundry allowance: ₱400/month
- Medical cash allowance for dependents: ₱2,000/semester
- Gifts (Christmas/anniversary): ₱6,000/year
- Meal allowance for OT/graveyard: 30% of regional minimum wage/day
- Achievement awards (tangible non-cash): ₱12,000/year
- CBA/productivity benefits: ₱12,000/year
- Monetized unused vacation leave: up to 12 days/year

## Category 3 — 13th month and other bonuses ceiling

Combined ceiling of **₱90,000/year** covers:
- Mandatory 13th month pay
- 14th month / Christmas bonus
- Mid-year bonus
- Productivity incentive pay
- Performance-based bonuses
- Other benefits of a similar nature

Amount above ₱90,000 is included in taxable compensation and taxed at graduated rates.

See full details in [`13th_month_pay.md`](13th_month_pay.md).

## Category 4 — Items that are NOT compensation at all

These are also not taxable because they are not considered compensation income:

| Item | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Employee reimbursements for business expenses (actual, receipted) | Not income — just a return of money spent for employer |
| Representation and transportation allowances (RATA) given to government officials | Special rule; fixed amounts per COA regulations |
| Loans and advances to employees | Not income (obligation to repay) |
| Employee-share of mandatory contributions (SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG) | Deducted BEFORE computing taxable income — not income in itself |

---

## What IS taxable (reference)

Everything not in the lists above:
- Basic salary above minimum wage
- Overtime pay of non-MWEs
- Bonuses, incentives above ₱90,000 ceiling
- Excess over de minimis ceilings
- Allowances that are not actual reimbursements
- Stock options / equity grants (separate FBT rules for management)
