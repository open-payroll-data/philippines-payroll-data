## Summary
Minimum wage is set **per region** by each Regional Tripartite Wages and Productivity Board (RTWPB), issued as numbered **Wage Orders** — there is **no single national rate and no fixed annual schedule**. The single golden source is the **NWPC "Latest Wage Orders" matrix**, which consolidates every region's current order. The table below is transcribed from the matrix **as of 04 May 2026**. NCR is the highest at **₱695/day** (Wage Order NCR-26, effective 18 July 2025).

> **This is the one payroll variable that changes continuously.** Each region issues on its own schedule (minimum 12 months between orders). Re-check the NWPC matrix before relying on any rate — see the monitoring note at the bottom.

> **Golden source:** transcribed from the NWPC matrix PDF (a digital PDF on the NWPC site). This file is `volatility: continuously-changing` — always re-pull the [latest NWPC matrix](https://nwpc.dole.gov.ph) before relying on a rate. **Read the two dates separately:** `last_verified` is when we last re-pulled the matrix; the data is only as current as `matrix_as_of` (**04 May 2026**). A new regional wage order can post any day after that, so a same-day `last_verified` does **not** mean a rate can't have changed — re-pull anyway.

## Key principle: Minimum wage earners are income-tax exempt
An employee earning at (or below) the applicable regional minimum wage for regular working days is **exempt from income tax** — including overtime, holiday pay, and night-shift differential (preserved under TRAIN Law). See [`../benefits/non_taxable_benefits.md`](../benefits/non_taxable_benefits.md).

## How minimum wage is used in payroll

### Daily rate → monthly equivalent
```
Monthly equivalent = Daily rate × 313 ÷ 12   (daily-paid, 6-day week)
   OR              = Daily rate × 261 ÷ 12   (5-day week)
   OR use the contractual monthly salary if the employee is monthly-paid
```

### Minimum Wage Earner (MWE) determination
An employee is an MWE if their basic daily wage equals the applicable regional minimum wage. MWEs are fully income-tax exempt (basic + OT + holiday + NSD).

### COLA
Some Wage Orders add a separate Cost of Living Allowance on top of the basic daily rate. The effective minimum = basic + COLA; COLA is also non-taxable for MWEs.
