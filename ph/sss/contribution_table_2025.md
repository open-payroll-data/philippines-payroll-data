## Summary
Per **SSS Circular No. 2024-006** (the primary issuing document), the total SSS contribution rate effective **1 January 2025** is **15%** of the monthly salary credit (MSC): employer **10%**, employee **5%**. MSC ranges from a **₱5,000 minimum to a ₱35,000 maximum**. Above an MSC of ₱20,000 the excess is channelled to the **Mandatory Provident Fund (MPF / "WISP")**. Employers separately pay an Employees' Compensation (EC) premium of ₱10 or ₱30.

> **This is the final scheduled rate increase under RA 11199.** The 15% rate is the last tranche of the contribution-rate schedule that began in 2019. There is **no 16% step and no 2027 increase** in the law — the circular repeals the prior (2023) schedule and sets 15% with no further legislated step. Any future change would require new legislation.

## How the MSC splits: Regular SS vs MPF (WISP)

- **MSC up to ₱20,000** → all goes to the **Regular SS Program** (the traditional benefits: retirement, disability, death, sickness, maternity, unemployment, funeral).
- **MSC from ₱20,000.01 to ₱35,000** → the **excess** (up to ₱15,000) goes to the **Mandatory Provident Fund (MPF)**, also called **WISP (Workers' Investment and Savings Program)** — a separate individual retirement-savings account that earns investment income.
- Both portions are still charged at the same 10% / 5% employer/employee split.

So a member at the ₱35,000 max contributes on ₱20,000 to Regular SS **plus** ₱15,000 to MPF/WISP.

## Contribution Table (representative brackets — verified from Circular 2024-006)

The full schedule lists every ₱500 bracket from ₱5,000 to ₱35,000. Representative rows:

| ₱9,750 – ₱10,249.99 | ₱10,000 | ₱10,000 | — | ₱500.00 | ₱1,000.00 | ₱10 | ₱1,510 |

| ₱24,750 – ₱25,249.99 | ₱25,000 | ₱20,000 | ₱5,000 | ₱1,250.00 | ₱2,500.00 | ₱30 | ₱3,780 |
| ₱29,750 – ₱30,249.99 | ₱30,000 | ₱20,000 | ₱10,000 | ₱1,500.00 | ₱3,000.00 | ₱30 | ₱4,530 |
| ₱34,750 and above | ₱35,000 | ₱20,000 | ₱15,000 | ₱1,750.00 | ₱3,500.00 | ₱30 | ₱5,280 |

Notes:
- "Employee" and "Employer" columns above combine the Regular SS and MPF portions (both at 5% / 10%).
- EC (₱10 or ₱30) is employer-paid and on top of the 15%.
- **The "Total (incl. EC)" column = Employee + Employer + EC.** So the pure 15% SSS contribution (EE + ER — what people usually mean by "the SSS contribution") is this Total **minus** the EC. Example at MSC ₱25,000: **₱3,780 incl. EC**, or **₱3,750 SSS-only** (EE ₱1,250 + ER ₱2,500).
- Any salary above ₱34,750 still uses ₱35,000 as the MSC (the ceiling).

## Remittance schedule

| Employer | Deadline | Channel |
|----------|----------|---------|
| All employers | On or before the last day of the month following the applicable month | SSS branch, SSS Payment Partners (SM, Bayad Centers, etc.), or SSS online (My.SSS) |

Example: January contributions are due on or before February 28. Some large employers have specific deadlines based on the 10th digit of their employer SSS number — check the circular.

## What counts as "compensation" for SSS
- Basic salary + regular allowances that are part of compensation
- Overtime, premium pay, and commissions are generally included in determining the MSC bracket
- If pay varies (e.g., commission-based), use actual cash pay for the month to determine the bracket
- **Domestic workers (Kasambahay):** different schedule — see Circular 2024-007.
