## Summary
**CPF** (Central Provident Fund) is Singapore's mandatory social-security/provident contribution, payable only for **Singapore Citizens and Permanent Residents** (NOT foreigners/work-pass holders). For employees aged **55 & below**, the total rate is **37%** = **17% employer + 20% employee**, on wages up to the **Ordinary Wage (OW) ceiling**: **$7,400/month in 2025**, rising to **$8,000/month from 1 Jan 2026**. Senior-worker rates are lower and rose on 1 Jan 2025 and again 1 Jan 2026. Separately, every employer pays the **Skills Development Levy (SDL)** — **0.25%** of monthly wages (min **$2**, max **$11.25**) — for **all** employees, **including foreigners**.

> Currency: Singapore Dollar (**S$** / `$`). Administrator: **CPF Board** (cpf.gov.sg), which also collects SDL on behalf of SkillsFuture Singapore.

## CPF contribution rates by age band

Apply to **SC / PR (3rd year onward)**, private-sector, on monthly wages **> $750**. Rates are **% of wages**; employer share = total − employee share.

### Effective 1 January 2025 (current through 31 Dec 2025)
| Employee age | Total % | Employer % | Employee % |
|--------------|---------|------------|------------|
| 55 & below | 37 | 17 | 20 |
| Above 55 to 60 | 32.5 | 15.5 | 17 |
| Above 60 to 65 | 23.5 | 12 | 11.5 |
| Above 65 to 70 | 16.5 | 9 | 7.5 |
| Above 70 | 12.5 | 7.5 | 5 |

OW ceiling **$7,400**. Max contribution on OW/month (55 & below): total **$2,738**, employee share **$1,480**.

### Effective 1 January 2026
| Employee age | Total % | Employer % | Employee % |
|--------------|---------|------------|------------|
| 55 & below | 37 | 17 | 20 |
| Above 55 to 60 | **34** | **16** | **18** |
| Above 60 to 65 | **25** | **12.5** | **12.5** |
| Above 65 to 70 | 16.5 | 9 | 7.5 |
| Above 70 | 12.5 | 7.5 | 5 |

OW ceiling **$8,000**. Max contribution on OW/month (55 & below): total **$2,960**, employee share **$1,600**.

**What changed on 1 Jan 2026:** only the two senior bands rose — *above 55–60* 32.5→**34%** and *above 60–65* 23.5→**25%**. The ≤55, 65–70 and >70 bands are unchanged.

### Already published: effective 1 January 2027
CPF Board has published the next senior-worker step (Budget 2025). Listed here as a heads-up — **use the 2025/2026 tables above for current payroll**:

| Employee age | Total % | Employer % | Employee % |
|--------------|---------|------------|------------|
| Above 55 to 60 | **35.5** | **16.5** | **19** |
| Above 60 to 65 | **26** | **13** | **13** |

Only these two bands change again; ≤55 (37%), 65–70 (16.5%) and >70 (12.5%) stay the same, and the **OW ceiling stays $8,000** (no further increase scheduled after 2026). Source: [CPF Board — contribution changes from 1 Jan 2027](https://www.cpf.gov.sg/employer/infohub/news/cpf-related-announcements/new-contribution-rates).

### Wage phase-in (low earners — both years)
- Monthly wages **≤ $50**: **no CPF**.
- **> $50 to $500**: **employer share only** (employer rate on total wages); no employee share.
- **> $500 to $750**: employer rate on total wages **plus a graduated employee share** (for 55 & below: employee share = 0.6 × (wages − $500)).
- **> $750**: full age-band rate above.

## Ordinary Wage (OW) ceiling schedule
The OW ceiling is the maximum monthly ordinary wage on which CPF is payable. It rose in four steps (Budget 2023):

| Effective period | OW ceiling |
|------------------|-----------|
| 1 Jan 2016 – 31 Aug 2023 | $6,000 |
| 1 Sep 2023 – 31 Dec 2023 | $6,300 |
| 1 Jan 2024 – 31 Dec 2024 | $6,800 |
| 1 Jan 2025 – 31 Dec 2025 | **$7,400** |
| 1 Jan 2026 onward | **$8,000** |

## Additional Wage (AW) ceiling
For bonuses and other additional wages, the yearly **AW ceiling = $102,000 − total Ordinary Wages subject to CPF for the year** (applied per employer per calendar year). **$102,000** is the CPF annual salary ceiling (max total OW + AW on which CPF is payable in a year) — unchanged for 2026.

## Who CPF applies to
- Payable for employees who are **Singapore Citizens or Permanent Residents** earning total wages **> $50/month**.
- **Foreigners / work-pass holders are NOT covered** — an employer cannot make CPF contributions for them even on request.
- **New PRs** pay reduced **graduated** rates in their 1st and 2nd year of PR status (e.g. ≤55, >$750: 1st year ≈ 9% employer + 5% employee; 2nd year ≈ 15% + 15%), reaching full rates from the **3rd year**.
- The employer may recover the **employee's** share from that month's wages; the employer's own share is the employer's liability.

## Skills Development Levy (SDL)
Separate from CPF. Payable by the employer for **all** employees (full-time, part-time, casual, temporary, **and foreigners** — work-permit and Employment Pass holders).

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Rate | **0.25%** of the employee's monthly total wages |
| Minimum | **$2** (employee earning ≤ $800/month) |
| Maximum | **$11.25** (employee earning > $4,500/month) |
| Wage cap | **$4,500** (no further SDL above this) |
| Rounding | Sum each employee's SDL, then round the **total down** to the nearest dollar |

> Key contrast: **CPF = SC/PR only; SDL = everyone, including foreigners.**

---

## Quick example (employee aged 40, monthly wage $5,000, 2025)
- Wage is below the $7,400 OW ceiling → CPF on the full $5,000.
- Total CPF = 37% × $5,000 = **$1,850**: employer **$850** (17%) + employee **$750** (20%, deducted from pay).
- SDL = 0.25% × $5,000 = $12.50 → capped at **$11.25** (wage > $4,500), employer-paid.
