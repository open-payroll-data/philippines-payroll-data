## Summary
Either party may end employment by giving **notice** (or paying **salary in lieu**). The statutory minimum notice (if the contract is silent) runs from **1 day** (< 26 weeks' service) to **4 weeks** (≥ 5 years). **Retrenchment benefit is NOT statutorily mandated** — the tripartite norm is **2 weeks to 1 month of salary per year of service** — but employers with **≥ 10 employees** who retrench **any** employee **must notify MOM within 5 working days**.

## Termination notice (if not specified in the contract)
| Length of service | Notice |
|-------------------|--------|
| < 26 weeks | 1 day |
| 26 weeks to < 2 years | 1 week |
| 2 to < 5 years | 2 weeks |
| 5 years or more | 4 weeks |

- Either party may pay **salary in lieu of notice**.
- The notice period **includes** the day notice is given, plus public holidays and rest days.

## Retrenchment
- **Retrenchment benefit is not mandated** by the Employment Act; it follows the contract / collective agreement and tripartite norms.
- **Eligibility norm:** employees with **≥ 2 years' service**; those with less may get an ex-gratia goodwill payment.
- **Prevailing quantum:** **2 weeks to 1 month of salary per year of service** (depends on the firm's finances and industry norm; unionised firms with a collective agreement: norm is 1 month per year).
- **Mandatory notification to MOM:** employers with **≥ 10 employees** that retrench **any** employee must notify MOM **within 5 working days** of informing the affected employee. (Employers with < 10 staff are encouraged, not required, to notify.)

## Annual Wage Supplement (AWS / "13th month")
**Not compulsory** in Singapore — AWS ("13th month payment") depends on the employment contract or collective agreement, not statute.
