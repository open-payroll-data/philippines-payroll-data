## Summary
The **Employment Act (EA)** covers all employees under a contract of service. Its **Part IV** protections (rest days, hours of work, **overtime pay**) apply only to **workmen earning ≤ $4,500/month** and **non-workmen earning ≤ $2,600/month** — managers/executives are EA-covered for core terms (salary, leave) but **not** Part IV. Overtime is paid at **≥1.5×** the hourly basic rate; for non-workmen the OT calculation is **capped at a $2,600 salary (hourly rate $13.60)**, up to **72 OT hours/month**.

## Employment Act coverage
- **Covered:** all employees under a contract of service — local and foreign, full-time, part-time, temporary, contract.
- **Excluded:** seafarers, domestic workers, statutory-board/civil-service employees.
- **Workman** = someone whose work is mainly **manual labour** (cleaner, construction worker, machine operator, driver, etc.; also a supervisor doing manual work > 50% of the time).

## Part IV — who gets rest days, hours limits, and overtime
| Employee type | Part IV applies if monthly basic salary is… |
|---------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Non-workman | **≤ $2,600** |
| Workman | **≤ $4,500** |
| Manager / executive | **Not covered** by Part IV (still EA-covered for salary, leave, etc.) |

## Overtime pay
- **Rate:** at least **1.5×** the employee's hourly basic rate.
- **Entitled:** Part IV employees only (workmen ≤ $4,500; non-workmen ≤ $2,600).
- **OT salary cap (non-workmen):** the OT calculation is capped at a **$2,600** monthly salary → **$13.60/hour** is the maximum hourly basic rate used.
- **Monthly OT limit:** up to **72 overtime hours** per month (more requires an exemption from MOM).
- **Hourly-rate formula (monthly-rated employee):** `hourly basic rate = (12 × monthly basic salary) ÷ (52 × 44)`; OT pay = hourly rate × 1.5 × OT hours.

---

## Quick example
Non-workman, monthly basic $2,400 (Part IV applies; below the $2,600 cap):
- Hourly rate = (12 × 2,400) ÷ (52 × 44) = 28,800 ÷ 2,288 = **$12.59**.
- OT pay per hour = $12.59 × 1.5 = **$18.88**.

Non-workman earning $5,000 → **not** Part IV → no statutory OT entitlement (OT is contractual only).
