## Summary
Singapore has **no universal statutory minimum wage**. Wage floors operate two ways: the **Progressive Wage Model (PWM)** sets sector-specific minimum wages (cleaning, security, landscape, retail, food services, waste management, lifts/escalators, administrators, drivers, and in-house roles), and the **Local Qualifying Salary (LQS)** — currently **$1,600/month** full-time (**$10.50/hr** part-time) — is the salary a local employee must be paid to count toward a firm's foreign-worker (Work Permit / S Pass) quota. **LQS rises to $1,800/month from 1 July 2026.**

> `volatility: continuously-changing` — PWM sector wage schedules step up on their own annual timetables and the LQS is periodically raised. For a specific sector's PWM floor or the current LQS, **re-confirm against the MOM page** (the sector PWM tables in particular are not all reproduced here).

## What applies
| Mechanism | What it is |
|-----------|-----------|
| Universal minimum wage | **None** in Singapore |
| Local Qualifying Salary (LQS) | Salary a local must earn to count toward the firm's Work Permit / S Pass quota. **$1,600/month full-time; $10.50/hour part-time. → $1,800/month full-time from 1 Jul 2026.** Applies to firms employing foreign workers. |
| Progressive Wage Model (PWM) | Sector-/job-specific minimum wage ladders. Covered sectors: cleaning, security, landscape, retail, food services, waste management, lifts & escalators, plus occupational PWM for administrators and drivers, and in-house roles. |

PWM + LQS together act as Singapore's de facto wage floor.
